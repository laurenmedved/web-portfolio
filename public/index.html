Why I Create

Thank you for taking the time to look through my website.

Most of my creative influence stems from my interests as a child. I loved to watch animated movies (some favorites include Bolt and WALL·E). I also grew up playing Nintendo games, particularly Pokemon. These interests exposed me to creative worlds. beautiful animations, and fascinating characters.

In my early years, I wanted to become an animator. However, after attending pre-college for animation at the Cleveland Institute of Art, I realized that it was not my ideal career path. I went on to explore computer science and I began creating more interactive work. During my senior year of high school, I created two games using Code.Org’s tools and throughout my first year of college I began to teach myself game development using Pico-8 and Godot. Although the world of video games fascinates me, I think I’d much prefer to leave it as a hobby over a career.

Throughout my first two years of college, I spent a lot of time exploring a variety of career interests based on my own passion and my percieved stability of the industry. Through several major switches and internships I was able to explore areas of computer science, information technology, marketing, and graphic design. Ultimately, I realized that my career path needs to be creative and that graphic design was a great fit for my future.
