Purple Dog

Branding and website created in Spring 2021.

Introduction

The goal of this project was to create an early education website that is accesible for children. My inspiration for this project was my two nephews, who love everything related to building and construction.

Branding

The branding that I created for Structured Education revolved around three colors, green, red, and yellow. The intent behind choosing these colors was to make it easier for children to recognize positive and negative concepts. Although I did not make any functioning games for this website, I had the concept that children would get green, yellow, and red messages depending on how they performed in the games. I also chose these colors because they're often seen throughout construction, such as with signage. In addition to these three colors, I added a warm gray to be a neutral color.

Along with these colors, I made a few textures that represented construction concepts, such as caution tape and steel beams. These textures are meant to add subsistence to basic designs throughout the website.

Personas

To begin with creating the website, I made three personas to represent the website's audience. I referenced these personas throughout the design process to make sure the website that I was creating was meeting their needs.

Website

I created a website prototype using Adobe XD. Before coding out the designs that I had made, I decided to perform some user testing. I met with three different families and had them sit down with their children to navigate the prototype. Through this user testing, I learned some pain points, such as when the kids wanted to enter the parent zone and the difficulty the children had understanding the paint brush icon.

Once I began coding, I had a lot of fun attempting to implement interactive features based on my knowledge of CSS and JavaScript. Below are two examples of interactive features that I included. The first one is an alphabet card flip that children can use to learn their ABCs. The other is a counting game, where children can click a button to see objects appear and move on the screen, along with a number counting these objects.
