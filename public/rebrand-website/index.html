Why I Create

Most of my creative influence stems from my interests as a child. I loved to watch animated movies (some favorites include Bolt and Spirited Away). I also grew up playing Nintendo games, particularly Pokemon. These interests exposed me to creative worlds. beautiful animations, and fascinating characters.

Throughout high school I spent time experimenting with illustrations and 3D models. My main career interest was in animation. However, after attending pre-college for animation at the Cleveland Institute of Art, I realized that it was not my ideal career path. I went on to explore computer science and I began creating more interactive work. During my senior year of high school, I created two games using Code.Orgâ€™s tools and throughout my first year of college I began to teach myself game development using Pico-8 and Godot.

At the beginning of college, I spent time exploring a variety of career interests based on my own passion and my percieved stability of the industry. Through several major switches and internships I was able to explore areas of computer science, information technology, marketing, and graphic design. Ultimately, I realized that I excel at logic and problem solving, but I also have a craving for creativity. I believe that my skills suit me best for a career involving web and UI/UX design, however I am always open to exploring new areas.
