Introduction

During the spring of 2022, I interned at Joyce Manufacturing, helping redesign their website as well as assisting with some other general graphic design needs. Unfortunately, my internship ended before the website could be fully launched. Below is the final screenshots before leaving the project.

Website Redesign

When I first started at Joyce, my main task was to redesign their current website at joycemfg.com using Wordpress. To begin the project, I began doing research on the tools that I could use to help make the process more efficient. I knew I only had the spring to work on this project, so I wanted to be able to make the final product as fast as I could. I presented several paid plugins to Joyce and eventually we decided to move forward using DiviBuilder.

After settling on how I would build the website, I began creating UI mockups using Adobe XD and kept in mind the limitations of the web builder I would be using. I went through a few different iterations of styles before we got down a design that the company wanted to move forward with. One of the most challenging aspects during the mockup phase was settling on cohesive branding- the company wanted the website to have little color and feel bright. I went with a navy blue for the main color so that it wouldn't be too playful, and then I made sure to use light gray and white on most of the main content. I also created some texture using a photograph of the vinyl used to make their products and used it as a dark background for some content. Beyond the website design, I also helped write and design content to be used on the website.

Once the mockups were approved, I began creating the website through Wordpress, using DiviBuilder. This process was relatively easy. Our biggest challenge was finding enough high quality product photos, and it often slowed down the actual build of the website. I created about 30 pages during my spring internship.
